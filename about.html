Why I'm doing this.

I spent two decades at the intersection of big-tech enterprise sales and emerging-market operations. Every quarter I watched founders in Riyadh, Dubai, Jeddah, and Amman try to build the same teams Silicon Valley builds, with the same playbooks, in a labor market that doesn't support it.

A competent operations hire in Dubai costs AED 15,000–25,000 per month and takes three months to onboard. A GTM team runs AED 40,000–80,000 per month. A dev team, AED 60,000–120,000. If you find the talent, they leave in 18 months. If you can't find them, you stay stuck.

The talent gap is structural. It's not going away. The answer isn't to hire harder — it's to install an organization that doesn't need the same team.

On January 1, 2026 I started building that organization inside SMOrchestra. Three core plugins (Cofounder, Tools Admin, Execution Scoring) plus two CMO add-ons (Social Media Operator, Campaign Operator). Claude as the brain. n8n as the nervous system. Every skill productized, every output scored, every role mapped.

By April 2026 the org was shipping 10 products in a single month. I wasn't managing 10 people. I was orchestrating 5 plugins.

What I believe.

  • The talent gap is structural. Hiring your way out is slower, more expensive, and riskier than installing an AI-Native Org. This is permanent, not cyclical.
